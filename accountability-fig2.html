Convergence finding (Thornton et al., 2012): Despite differences in dominant institutional logics — Industrial/Civic/Market (EU), State/Market/Professional (Colorado), Industrial/State-Developmental (India), and State-Developmental/Civic (Brazil) — all four jurisdictions exhibit the same structural pattern: accountability flows upward through compliance architecture. None establishes enforceable mechanisms through which impacted communities can meaningfully influence algorithmic outcomes. Brazil's civic logic comes closest to empowerment on paper, but its enforcement infrastructure mirrors the same upward-flowing compliance pattern observed elsewhere.
